## 想法、思考与表达

一个想法或一种认识，在没有具象成文字或到可真正传达的程度之前，它只是一种更高维度的存在，且无法被其他任何事物乃至自己所理解或意识，如同一个微弱的信号在人们脑海中呢喃，却难以描述它为何物以及为什么会出现，有时以潜在意识的方式所体现，让人自身都难以察觉、知晓。只有当对它的感受达到了一种能够开口说出或写下来的地步，这种高维存在才降维到了一种低纬、低秩的可理解状态，进而真正推动语言输出与行为表达。

在这个感受的过程中，或者说是由高维到低维的降维过程中，我们努力提取出有关那个想法和认识的主要结构特征或最重要的方向。在高维度的抽象语义空间里，从一开始的无法直观理解，到花时间慢慢去捕捉、构建和形成能够有序表达、推演，并且符合其逻辑的那个典型部分，进行不断补充和完善，最终产出一个可以用更低维替代高维进行的清晰表达。所谓的思考，其实就是围绕这个想法所进行的降维活动。想法越复杂，维度越高，就越必要进行降维。

任何一个想法都可以通过这样的思考，得到一个确定性的表达。而要产生一个尚不存在的表达，除了抽象的想法和思考，更为关键的是：不断地表达。

## SVD

线性代数中的SVD其实是一个非常实用的工具，把任意一个矩阵分解成3个矩阵的乘积，也就是左、右奇异向量矩阵(的转置），和中间的奇异值向量矩阵。它是一种大道至简的“自然设计”，其应用也可以说是非常广泛！

比如它可以用很简单的方式解决了CV中的人脸识别问题。对人脸图像进行批量处理，可以发现每个人都有一张平均脸（不论是否化妆，拍摄角度，表情如何）和特征脸（即相对平均脸差异化表现），可以以一种富有成效的方式极大地忽略一张人脸图像中的噪音和冗余信息。

对于CFD而言，一个例子就是把每一个时刻下的云图的screenshot 作为原始数据，将瞬时的单张图片信息写成一个列向量，表示这个时刻的整个流场状态，而行向量为某个像素信息（也就是空间点）在所有时间下的变化（即一条随时间变化的曲线）。一张流场的contour通过SVD可以依次（按照奇异值大小进行重要性选择）得到主导流动结构的特征，如稳态结构，以及次级结构或噪音，如涡摆结构等等。这一点和傅立叶变换分解有些类似，但本质仍有所不同，SVD是自适应分解，基底完全取决于数据。

那它还能干吗？其实，它就像是一个能让数据说话的扬声器，针对任何能够表示成一个数字矩阵的对象或数据集，并期望从中发现有潜在规律、模式、配置，它都能更好地发挥出大量数据本该提供的内在价值，进而指导决策。

## 时间换空间（时间对称性）

假设一个人的生命只有10天。从第一天开始，他就必须依靠劳动去换取当天的食物与住所。他能活多久，并不完全取决于时间本身，而取决于他如何在时间里安排劳动与消耗，以及是否为之后的日子提前留下余量。

最直接的方式，是工作一天，换取一天的生存资源，一直持续到第十天，生命也随之结束。

但如果有一天，他的劳动可以换来两天的生存资源，那么事情就开始不一样了。他只需要工作五天，就能覆盖全部十天的生存需求。剩下的五天，便不再必须为了活下去而工作。这时发生变化的，其实不是收入，而是时间本身。前五天，是被生存推动的时间；后五天，才开始出现可以自行支配的时间。

要做到这一点，路径其实很简单：要么提高获取资源的能力，要么降低维持生存的成本。当这两件事同时发生，一个人就能够把原本分散在每天的生存压力，逐渐提前，集中到生命的前半段。

于是，一种新的结构慢慢出现——生命开始呈现出某种对称性：前半段承担必要性，用于交换生存，后半段获得选择，才有空间思考意义。

也许人与人之间真正的差别，并不在于拥有多少时间，而在于是否能够更早地解决关于时间的问题。当生存不再占满全部时间时，人才第一次真正开始拥有属于自己时间的空间。

## Do 和 Implement

Nike 的 “Just do it” 确实有它的价值：很多时候，人确实需要先动起来，先跨过犹豫本身。但问题在于，很多人把 Do 理解成了不加区分地去试、去撞、去碰运气。这样一来，Do 就更像“赌”——在没有方向、没有结构、也缺少反馈校正的情况下，到处支出时间、精力，乃至牺牲身体健康。

但一旦你在不断的尝试和反馈中，逐渐看清了一个值得投入的方向，事情就不该停留在 Do，而该进入 Implement。

有意思的是，Implement 这个词的中文发音很像“硬扑里面”。这个谐音其实很贴切：真正的 implement，从来不只是做一下，而是往一个方向硬扎进去，让想法开始在现实中收敛、沉淀成结构，而不是停留在发散性的“赌”上。

## Book Your Future

Several days ago, I went to a massage shop to relax. During the conversation, I told one of the staff members that I was planning to write a book called The Worker Investor.

The idea comes from the reality I am living in right now.

Like every other worker, I have a payday. At the end of each month, the company pays me for the time I have already sold.

But one question kept coming back to me:

If you already knew how to invest your money, what else would you invest?

My answer is simple: you should invest in yourself.

Financial capital matters, but human capital matters more. The most important asset is not only the money in your brokerage account. The most important asset is you: your time, your energy, your skills, your judgment, your body, your attention, your ability to create, and your ability to keep learning.

Many workers focus only on earning and spending. But I do not think we should limit ourselves to waiting for the next payment from an employer.

You can read.
You can write.
You can build a skill.
You can create a project.
You can study investing.
You can improve your health.
You can record your thoughts.
You can build an audience.
You can turn your experience into an asset.

That is the difference between a worker and a worker investor.

A worker exchanges time for money. A worker investor still works, but also uses time to build assets that may continue creating value in the future. He asks a different question:

Besides earning this month’s salary, what can I build this month that may still serve me in the future?

When I asked the staff member what she usually does outside of work, she said she would just work hard and wait for the next payment.

I immediately told her, “No. You have to change your mindset.”

Of course, working hard matters. But if work is only a way to transform time into money, then money is all you will receive. You may get paid, but you may not grow. You may survive, but you may not accumulate. You may become tired, but not necessarily stronger.

The worker investor thinks differently.

He understands that time, attention, and energy are resources to invest, not just spend. Some of them must be used to earn a living. But some should also be used to build a future.

This book is not about how to make 100% returns in the stock market. It is not about finding a perfect portfolio or becoming rich overnight.

It is about something more basic and more difficult:

How do you stop being only a seller of time, and start becoming an investor of your own future?

That is the real question behind The Worker Investor.

Because in the end, payday gives you money for the past.

But what you do with your time determines your future.

## Compounding Yourself

In my first year of working, I learned how to operate inside the real engineering and automotive industry. I learned how organizations work, how projects move, how responsibilities are distributed, and how a young engineer should survive in a complex industrial system.

At the same time, I began to learn the basic logic of saving and investing. I learned that money should not simply be earned and spent. It should be saved, allocated, and allowed to compound.

But by the middle of my second year, I realized something more important:

The most important asset is not money. The most important asset is oneself.

More precisely, it is the human being behind the money: the person’s thinking, judgment, understanding, discipline, energy, and ability to make decisions.

Money is only a number. What truly matters is how you understand that number, how you use it, and what you are willing to exchange it for.

Because in the end, money is not only used to buy material things. It is also connected to non-material things:

1. Health — what you are willing to pay to protect your body and energy.
2. Time — what you are willing to pay to regain freedom and optionality.
3. Wisdom — what you are willing to invest in learning, books, experience, and better judgment.
4. Happiness — what kind of life your money is helping you build.
5. Peace — whether money reduces anxiety or creates more desire.
6. Relationships — whether money supports love, family, friendship, and trust.
7. Value — whether money helps you create something meaningful beyond yourself.

So the real question is not only:

How much money do I have?

The deeper question is:

What kind of person is this money forming me into?

This is the transition from financial investing to human investing.

A worker asks:

How much did I earn this month?

A worker investor asks:

What did this month of work, saving, learning, and thinking turn me into?

That is why the real portfolio is not only in a brokerage account.

The real portfolio is the self.

## Viability

In my first year after graduate school, I learned how to survive inside the real engineering and automotive industry. I also learned how to save, invest, and think about financial assets.

But by the middle of my second year, I realized something deeper:

The most important portfolio is not SPY, QQQ, or any financial ETF.

The most important portfolio is myself.

A salary can give me cash flow, but it cannot satisfy my desire for long-term growth. I do not only want my money to compound. I want my knowledge, judgment, health, time, wisdom, relationships, and inner clarity to compound as well.

That is why I started thinking about the idea of a Human Capital ETF: dividing the self into different layers — core, growth, distribution, and meta — so that human capital can be understood, managed, and improved more clearly.

Then came another idea: The Worker Investor.

A worker sells time for salary.
A worker investor uses salary, work experience, knowledge, and pain as raw material for future assets.

This is not a miracle story about getting rich. It is an ordinary person’s path from simply working to consciously investing in the self.

The third idea is Before the Genius: studying the early lives, childhoods, training systems, and formative environments of people like Newton, Elon Musk, and other figures in history — not to worship genius, but to understand how human potential is formed.

The fourth idea brings these thoughts together: Compounding Yourself. It’s a mathematical concept applied to human life itself.

Compounding means doing the right things consistently and patiently, especially when you have very little. At the beginning, the results may seem almost invisible. You invest a small amount of time, energy, attention, or effort, and nothing appears to change. But if you keep doing the right things, time eventually starts to work for you. One day, what began as a small portion may return far more than everything you once paid into it.

Compounding is important, but yourself is more important.

When you have little money, the only thing you can truly compound is yourself. And even if you eventually have a amount of money, it ultimately means little if you stopped compounding yourself.

In the era of AI, I have more leverage to think, to research, and to communicate with multiple advanced neural networks. I came up with these four concepts, each symbolizing one website that I registered:

humancapitaletf.com
workerinvestor.com
beforethegenius.com
compoundingyourself.com

They are like four children needing me to nourish them to grow: to feed them the arguments, evidence, and references to transfer that information into the formation of themselves.

At the very early days of my working life, I didn't have such an idea and I didn't know what to do. But over 1.5 years of working and constantly thinking, I managed to find something that is worthy to do in the long run.

## 在班上

黑塞的 Beneath the Wheel /《在轮下》 原作出版于 1906 年，核心人物 Hans Giebenrath 被整个小镇和学校推上“优秀学生”的轨道，学习、考试、纪律和期待不断压缩他的个人发展空间。Macmillan 对这本书的介绍也强调，Hans 的成功只换来了更多沉重的任务，并最终在学术体系的灰色同质化中崩溃。

如果说学校不是压迫的终点，而是现代劳动社会的预演，那么《在轮下》写的是人如何在教育机器中被压碎；而与此相对应的《在班上》，写的是人如何在劳动机器中被持续磨损、消耗。

显然，《在班上》可以有更深的含义：既能指公司里的“班”，也能表示教室里的“班”。它就像一根藤蔓，从不见光日的地表下找着方向破开土，终于爬了出来，然后呢？请持续努力，继续往上爬。这或许就是《在班上》的全部含义。

在这里，《在班上》尽管只是一个轻佻的书名调侃，但结合《在轮下》带给我们的思考，它也许就是：即便选择了顽强活下去的汉斯，在成年人社会里终究会持续面对的、尚未被充分书写的精神困难。

## Looking for Mr. Success

People look for Mr. Success as if success were a person. They think that if they can meet him, work with him, follow him, invest with him, or simply stand close to him, their own life will change.

But success is not a person.

It is not an object either. You cannot hold “success” in your hand. Money, status, job titles, followers, awards — these may be signs of success, but they are not success itself.

More precisely, success is an event. It is the moment when a long, invisible process finally becomes visible. A company goes public. A book becomes known. A person gets promoted. A portfolio crosses a certain number. A creator gains an audience. From the outside, people say, “He succeeded.”

But the person did not live in that moment. He endured the process before the moment.

He lived through in the years of learning, saving, building, failing, adjusting, publishing, investing, and staying in the game. The event is what people see. The process is what produced it.

So the real question is not: How do I find Mr. Success and to be successful?

The real question is: What process must I go through so that one day, from the outside, people may call the result success?

## AI And I

当下最受追捧的是 AI，AI，还是 AI。LLMs 作为一种新兴的、如今最为通用、最有价值的信息媒介，在它真正大众化的头几年里，已经深刻改变了信息的生产、组织与分发方式（从过去一年Saas公司股价一路下跌可以看出市场的惊慌）。但很多变化并不是像闪电划过黑夜那样瞬息可见，并短暂地留下一台延时录音机，放着一段霹雳贯耳的回声进行二次提醒，而更像是对信息的熵进行重分布：一边将个体获取答案、组织语言、内容制作的成本无限拉低，每个人都能从人类所构建的AI共识参数里获得信息的降熵处理；而另一边，当每个人都不断地分发、参与创造新的共识时，又会持续地让由数以万亿计的token编织而成的信息之网变得更加拥挤、冗余、嘈杂和不确定。它就在这样慢慢渗透进我们的阅读、写作、工作和娱乐之中，参与人类关于文化、生产力工具的讨论，乃至开始重塑人类社会所有有关AI的共识定义。

我不得不说，这是一个效率通胀的时代，也是一个注意力通缩的时代，如今我们所拥有的，已远超我们所必要的。从远处看，我们最终会得到一片热寂。而眼下，即便是有迫切性的实质需求，也只是更加努力地试图说明了这一点而已。

试想，LLMs再强，也不能替一个人完成他自己的使命（更别说宇宙的使命了）。

我们利用LLMs：
总结一本书，代替不了他/她感受阅读；
阅读，代替不了他/她缓缓地思考；
思考，代替不了他/她行动；
行动，也就是最为考验人的地方，绝不仅仅是行为本身的单一重复输出、劳动，而是能否在一次次真实事件中完成累积、修正、反馈和迭代。

AI时代，真正能改变人的，不是旨在成为工具本身，或沦为工具奴役的对象，而是一个人如何使用工具，审慎地组织自己的注意力、判断和执行，持续对抗、改造我们所身处的这个熵减世界。
